# Extending the five-route KK result — how far the mathematics can go

*Discussion note, 2026-07-22. Not a leg; a map for later. Captures the honest ceiling on extending leg S/U
(the KK mass tower + axion, established five independent ways) to richer settings. The organizing idea: two
completely different meanings of "more complex universe," with opposite answers.*

## The distinction that IS the answer

"Extend this to more complex universes" can mean either of two things:

- **(A) Richer mathematics** — curved hidden spaces, more fields, self-responding geometry. This is a
  **ladder**: climbable rung by rung; the walls are *compute* walls.
- **(B) Closer to our actual universe** — "is this how reality works?" This is a **cliff**, not a ladder,
  and it is **categorical, not incremental** — no amount of compute or cleverness crosses it.

Popular coverage conflates them; the whole honesty of the result lives in keeping them apart.

## (A) The mathematical ladder — climbable, several rungs

| rung | what changes | status / reach |
|---|---|---|
| 0 (done) | flat hidden torus, free scalar wave | leg S (5D), leg U (6D T² + axion). Five routes agree. |
| 1 | **curved hidden space** (sphere S³/S⁴ instead of flat torus) — tower becomes Casimir `n(n+2)`, not sum-of-squares | **Clear next leg.** rest-buzz works on any hidden manifold whose Laplacian is computable; the audited G₂ paper used S³×S⁴. Concrete, gateable, keeps all five routes. |
| 2 | **more fields → charge** (the photon falls out of 5D gravity; non-abelian charge from a sphere's SU(2)) | Partly done: ansatz §111 (KK photon); tabula discovered "charge" as a latent (Phase D). Non-abelian is harder but mapped. |
| 3 | **backreaction / moduli stabilization** — does the hidden dimension *stay* the size you set? | The frontier the family already touches: flux atlas §114 pins the hidden volume dynamically. Hard (string-adjacent) but has a toehold. |
| 4 | **realistic matter** — chiral fermions, 3 generations, the SM gauge group | **Hard wall, and everyone's wall.** Witten's no-go: no chiral fermions from a smooth KK reduction without extra structure. Not a compute wall we could out-muscle; a stuck research frontier. |

**Verdict on (A):** genuinely climbable for ~3 more rungs (curved space → richer fields → backreaction),
each a legitimate leg that keeps the five-route pattern and makes the object *richer*, not repetitive. Then
it hits the same wall the professionals hit.

## (B) The cliff — two layers, both categorical

1. **The evidence wall.** If hidden dimensions exist, they sit at ~10¹⁵–10¹⁶ GeV (the audited paper's
   M_KK ≈ 5×10¹⁵ GeV is typical). The LHC reaches ~10⁴ GeV — the relevant physics is ~10¹² times beyond any
   machine we can build. And the family, by construction, has **no instrument touching reality** at that
   scale. Crossing from "consistent math" to "true of our universe" requires evidence the family
   structurally cannot generate.
2. **The landscape wall (bites even inside pure math).** There isn't *one* way to hide dimensions — ~10⁵⁰⁰
   consistent ways (the string landscape), each giving different physics, with **no known principle to pick
   ours.** So even with infinite compute and no experiment, "*which* hidden geometry is real?" is
   **undetermined — by anyone, not just us.** The math offers a near-infinite menu; it does not select a
   universe.

**Verdict on (B):** you can make the mathematical object as rich and internally consistent as you like, and
it never becomes a statement about our world — because the one thing that would connect it (data at 10¹⁶
GeV) is unreachable, and the math itself does not narrow the menu.

## What is actually realistic to do next

- **Best concrete leg:** the **tower on a curved hidden space** (S³) — concrete, gateable, keeps all five
  routes, generalizes the crown jewel to the setting real compactifications use. This is a real (A)-sense
  extension within reach.
- **Most extensible route:** **route 5** (tabula's neural discovery) — because it is a *method*, not a
  calculation: "can a learner recover hidden structure it was never shown?" generalizes to any hidden
  geometry, any dimension. (Separate note: `ROUTE5_NOTES.md`.)
- **Not realistic, stated plainly:** any claim about our actual universe — not for lack of skill, but
  because the evidence is ~12 orders out of reach and the math offers 10⁵⁰⁰ options with no selector.

## The bottom line

The five-route result is strong *because* it is a statement about the **internal consistency of a
mathematical idea**, stress-tested five independent ways. Extending it up the mathematical ladder makes that
object richer and even more consistent — several rungs are in reach. But "richer and more consistent" never
quietly upgrades to "true of our universe": that gap is not a compute problem, it is the gap between
mathematics and physics, guarded by a wall no simulation crosses. The crown jewel is a jewel of
**consistency**, not of **discovery** — and keeping those two words apart is the whole art.

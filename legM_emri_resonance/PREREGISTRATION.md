# Leg M — Pre-registration: the observational handle on leg J (backlog B1 + A3)

*leg J: the bump kills the exact Carter constant but the motion is KAM-regular. Question: what would an EMRI
(LISA) observation see? B1's hypothesis: broken integrability → resonance-crossing glitches. A3: is there
chaos AT a resonance that the fine scan stepped over?*

## Method
Compute orbital frequencies ω_r (radial), ω_θ (latitudinal) from libration periods of geodesics in the
bump vs Kerr; locate low-order resonances (ω_r/ω_θ = n:m). At a resonance, compare Carter-constant drift and
Lyapunov exponent on- vs off-resonance, bump vs Kerr. Calibrate on Kerr (frequencies smooth, C₀ conserved).

## Frozen predictions
- **Frequency map (B1):** the bump shifts ω_r, ω_θ and the resonance locations vs Kerr — a frequency-domain
  observable, independent of chaos. (Direction not assumed.)
- **A3 (chaos at the resonance):** EITHER the resonance shows enhanced Carter drift / positive Lyapunov
  (broken integrability bites there — a chaotic-glitch EMRI signature) OR it is a regular resonance (λ at
  the Kerr floor, drift tracking eccentricity not the resonance), consistent with leg J's robust regularity.
  Both are real outcomes; no result assumed.
- **Honest scope registered:** a full EMRI *waveform* needs GW fluxes (radiation reaction) — out of scope
  here; this establishes the geodesic frequency map and the resonance character, the precondition for a
  waveform study.
